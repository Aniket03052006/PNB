THE PIPELINE

From Domain to CBOM in Five Phases

Every scan runs through a deterministic five-phase pipeline grounded in NIST FIPS 203, 204, and 205.

Phase 1

Asset Discovery

Discovers domains, subdomains, IPs, SSL certificates, and software across your bank's attack surface using passive and active enumeration.

Phase 2

Tri-Mode TLS Probing

Three simultaneous probes simulate PQC-capable, classical TLS 1.3, and legacy TLS 1.2 clients. Captures what each client type actually negotiates.

Phase 3

PQC Classification

Q-Score engine evaluates six dimensions: TLS version, key exchange, certificate algorithm, cipher strength, crypto-agility, and negotiation policy.

Phase 4

CBOM Generation

Produces a CycloneDX 1.7 Cryptographic Bill of Materials with Ed25519 signed attestation for every scanned asset.

Phase 5

Certification Labeling

Issues Elite-PQC, Standard, or Legacy labels. Revocable registry with public verification endpoint. Alerts on regression.

CAPABILITIES

Everything a bank needs to go quantum-safe

Negotiation Policy Analyzer

Detects what each client tier actually negotiates — not just what the server supports. Identifies downgrade vulnerabilities invisible to standard scanners.

CycloneDX 1.7 CBOM

Generates spec-compliant Cryptographic Bill of Materials with key length distribution, cipher usage, CA breakdown, and Ed25519-signed attestation.

Security Posture Heatmap

3×3 risk grid showing PQC readiness vs negotiation strength. Click any cell to drill into assets. Migration arrow shows the path to Elite-PQC.

Enterprise Cyber Rating

Aggregated 0–1000 score across all assets. Tier thresholds: Legacy <400, Standard 400–700, Elite-PQC >700. Mapped to NIST compliance criteria.

Regression Detection

Compares every scan to history. Alerts on Q-Score drops ≥5, algorithm downgrades, new vulnerable assets, and negotiation tier regressions.

Harvest-Now-Decrypt-Later Detection

Identifies assets where legacy client traffic can be captured today and decrypted when quantum computers mature. Direct threat model mapping.
